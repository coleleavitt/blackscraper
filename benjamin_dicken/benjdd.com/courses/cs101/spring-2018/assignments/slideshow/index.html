CSc 101: Slideshow

In this assignment, you’ll create a slide-show application based on a topic of your choosing. One example of such a slide-show is shown below:

Notice a few things that you must conform to when you write your program:

  • The slide-show has a specific theme. In this example, the theme is “the avengers” but you may choose whatever theme you like (must be PG-rated).
  • There are four “slides” total. You must have at least 4, but you may also create more.
  • Each slide has a title (with large text), an image, and a short description (smaller text).
  • You can also navigate left/right by using the ‘z’ and ‘m’ keys, respectively.
  • There is no “wraparound” when browsing through slides. You may only navigate forward/backward if there are more slides in that direction.
  • The background of your entire canvas should also be an image that goes with the theme.
  • The canvas should be 700x500
  • Extra Credit: You may navigate through the slides by clicking on the left and right arrow buttons on either edge of the screen.

The only code you are allowed to put into draw() are calls to functions that you have written. You may not draw any shapes, respond to mouse clicks, have if-statements or for loops, etc. within draw(). Instead, put your code into at least three separate, well-named functions.

Development Strategy

As with the last assignment, this one might seem complex. If you break the problem down into smaller “chunks” it becomes much more manageable. I recommend you use the following strategy to complete:

(1) Choosing a theme

First, choose a theme that has at least 4 distinct sub-topics that you can create a slide about. Then, choose your 5 images (1 for the background, and 4 for the slides) and put them in the same directory as your program.

(2) Background Image

Next, create your canvas and get the background image displayed and properly scaling across the screen.
