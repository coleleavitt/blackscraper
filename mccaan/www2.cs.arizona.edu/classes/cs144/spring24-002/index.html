Reference Materials       (Pointers to additional information)
  • The text for this semester is Discrete Mathematics and Its Applications, 8th ed., by Kenneth H. Rosen, 2019.
    • Do not buy an international edition or an earlier edition! Instead...
    • This book is available as an ebook via the Pay One Price program, or you can also get it from the publisher as an ebook or in print.
    • You might also wish to consider purchasing the companion Student Solutions Guide, which offers detailed solutions to the text's odd-numbered end-of-section exercises.
  • Kneel Before Zodd is a draft of several chapters (and an appendix) that I've written to cover my lecture material through roughly the first half of the old CSc 245 course. Much of it is also applicable to this class.
    • Access info is available on Piazza.
    • Prefer to access the chapters individually? Here are links to separate PDF files for each chapter (and the appendix):
      • Chapter 1: Logic
      • Chapter 2: Quantified Expressions
      • Chapter 3: Logical Arguments
      • Chapter 4: Direct Proofs
      • Chapter 5: Indirect ("Contra") Proofs
      • Chapter 6: Additional Set Concepts
      • Chapter 7: Matrices
      • Chapter 8: Relations
      • Appendix A: Math Review
  • There are some other online textbooks that cover parts of the material in this class:
    • Connecting Discrete Mathematics and Computer Science by David Liben-Nowell. This is a pre-print version a now-published textbook.
    • "Book of Proof" by Richard Hammack. The name's a bit misleading; it covers a good amount of the material for a discrete structures course, including proofs.
    • An Active Introduction to Discrete Mathematics and Algorithms (V3.0) by Charles A. Cusack. A discrete math text based on parts of another author's writings.
    • Introduction to Higher Mathematics by Patrick Keef and David Guichard. Logic, proofs, functions, and relations, plus some number theory, 'cause ... why not?
    • How To Write Proofs by Larry W. Cusick. And that's what it covers.
  • LaTeX resources:
    • I've got a resource page for LaTeX for those of you who would like to use it for formatting your answers to homework assignments. (Last updated: July 2021)
    • Don't want to install LaTeX on your computer? Try Overleaf, a free (for personal use) online LaTeX editor.
    • Want help formatting equations in LaTeX? You might like this tool: LaTeX equation demo by MyScript
